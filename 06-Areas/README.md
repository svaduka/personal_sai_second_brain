# 🔄 Areas of Responsibility

Areas are ongoing domains of life or work that require continuous attention and maintenance. Unlike projects, areas don't have an end date—they represent ongoing standards you want to maintain.

## Purpose

- **Maintain standards**: Track ongoing responsibilities
- **Organize long-term commitments**: Career, health, relationships, etc.
- **Enable review**: Regular check-ins on important life areas
- **Context for projects**: Projects often support maintaining areas

## Area vs. Project

### Key Differences

| Aspect | Project | Area |
|--------|---------|------|
| **Timeline** | Temporary (has end date) | Ongoing (no end date) |
| **Goal** | Specific outcome | Maintain a standard |
| **Completion** | Can be "done" | Never truly "done" |
| **Status** | Active/Complete/Cancelled | Always active |
| **Examples** | "Renovate kitchen" | "Home & living" |
| **Success** | Delivered on time | Standard maintained |

### Examples Compared

**Projects** (temporary, outcome-based):
- Launch new website
- Learn Python
- Write book
- Plan vacation
- Organize home office

**Areas** (ongoing, standard-based):
- Career development
- Health & fitness
- Family relationships
- Personal finances
- Home maintenance

## When to Use Areas

✅ **Use Areas for:**
- Ongoing responsibilities at work
- Life domains requiring continuous attention
- Long-term commitments
- Standards you want to maintain
- Roles you hold (parent, manager, team member)

❌ **Don't use Areas for:**
- Temporary work with clear endpoints (use Projects)
- One-time goals (use Projects)
- General knowledge (use Permanent notes)
- Reference materials (use Resources)

## Common Areas

### Personal Life
- **Health & Fitness**: Exercise, nutrition, sleep, mental health
- **Personal Finance**: Budgeting, investments, taxes
- **Relationships**: Family, friends, partner
- **Personal Growth**: Learning, skills, hobbies
- **Home & Living**: Maintenance, organization, comfort

### Professional Life
- **Career Development**: Skills, networking, growth
- **Team Management**: If you manage people
- **Product/Project Ownership**: If you own a domain
- **Professional Learning**: Staying current in field
- **Side Business**: If you have one

### Creative/Other
- **Writing**: If you write regularly
- **Content Creation**: Blogging, videos, podcasts
- **Community Involvement**: Volunteering, groups
- **Hobbies**: Photography, music, sports, etc.

## Area Workflows

### Weekly Area Review

**Quick check-in (5 minutes per area):**
- [ ] Is the standard being maintained?
- [ ] Any urgent matters to address?
- [ ] Update active projects list
- [ ] Note any concerns or wins

### Monthly Area Review

**Deeper review (15-30 minutes per area):**
- [ ] Review standard—still appropriate?
- [ ] Check progress on metrics
- [ ] Identify what's working/not working
- [ ] Plan any new projects to improve this area
- [ ] Update resources or links
- [ ] Celebrate wins, adjust as needed

### Quarterly Area Review

**Strategic assessment:**
- [ ] Is this area getting enough attention?
- [ ] Should standards be raised or adjusted?
- [ ] Any systemic improvements needed?
- [ ] Archive completed projects
- [ ] Plan projects for next quarter

### Naming Convention

**Format**: `Area-Name.md` or `Area-Name/`

**Examples:**
- `Health-Fitness.md`
- `Career-Development.md`
- `Home-Living.md`

**Use hyphens**, not spaces or underscores

## Common Mistakes

❌ **Too many areas**
- Trying to track 20+ areas → overwhelming
- ✅ Limit to 5-8 key areas

❌ **Treating projects as areas**
- "Launch product" is a project, not an area
- ✅ "Product management" could be an area

❌ **Never reviewing areas**
- Areas drift without regular attention
- ✅ Monthly review keeps them healthy

❌ **Areas without clear standards**
- "Fitness" is vague
- ✅ "Exercise 3x/week, 7h sleep" is clear

❌ **Perfectionism in standards**
- Setting unrealistic standards
- ✅ Start with achievable, adjust upward

## Area Health Indicators

### Healthy Area
✅ Standard is clear and achievable
✅ Reviewed monthly
✅ Spawns occasional improvement projects
✅ Metrics tracked (if applicable)
✅ Feels sustainable

### Struggling Area
⚠️ Standard hasn't been met in months
⚠️ Not reviewed in 3+ months
⚠️ Feels overwhelming or neglected
⚠️ Constant firefighting, no improvements

**If an area is struggling:**
1. **Lower the standard** temporarily (better to maintain low than fail at high)
2. **Start a project** to systematically improve it
3. **Get support** (accountability, help, resources)
4. **Re-evaluate** if this area truly needs attention now

## Areas and Second Brain Integration

**Areas inform knowledge base:**
- Ongoing work generates insights
- Extract learnings to permanent notes
- Create resources from area experience

**Knowledge base supports areas:**
- Permanent notes inform standards
- Resources provide systems for areas
- MOCs provide overview of relevant knowledge

**Cycle:**
```
Area → Experience → Insights → Permanent Notes
     ← Informed by ← Knowledge ←
```

## Quick Start

**Setting up your first area:**
1. **Identify 3-5 key areas** in your life
2. **Create simple note** for each area
3. **Define clear standard** for each (what does "healthy" look like?)
4. **List active projects** supporting each area
5. **Schedule monthly review** for each area

**Start minimal**—add complexity only as needed.

---

**Remember**: Areas are the ongoing rhythms of life. They don't need to be perfect—they need to be maintained. Use projects to improve areas systematically over time. The goal is sustainable standards, not constant optimization.

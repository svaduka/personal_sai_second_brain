# Novels to Short Films — YouTube Channel

## Context

- **Origin**: Personal insight
- **Date captured**: 2026-06-16
- **Why now**: AI video generation tools are maturing — converting written stories into visual content is becoming feasible at low cost

## Main Idea

Take novels (classic or popular) and convert them into short films published on YouTube. Each novel becomes a series of short episodes — making literature accessible, visual, and consumable for audiences who prefer watching over reading.

## Supporting Details

- Novels provide unlimited free/public-domain source material (classics)
- Short film format suits YouTube consumption habits (10-20 min episodes)
- A single novel can produce an entire season of content
- AI tools can assist with: script adaptation, voice narration, image/video generation, editing
- Revenue through YouTube monetization, sponsorships, Patreon

## Questions

- Copyright: stick to public domain novels first, or license modern ones?
- Format: live action, animation, AI-generated visuals, or narration with visuals?
- Episode length: 5 min? 10 min? 20 min?
- Which genre performs best on YouTube — thriller, romance, sci-fi, mythology?
- Narration language: English, Telugu, Hindi, multi-language?
- AI tools to use: Sora, Runway, ElevenLabs for voice?
- How to differentiate from existing book summary channels?
